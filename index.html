An AI assistant that actually gets to know you, and stays under your control.

EmiOS learns your routine, your people, your habits, your sleep, your work, and acts on that. All the data lives on your computer. Risky actions hit approval gates. The general assistant can read your knowledge graph but cannot change it. Built like an operating system: rooms as workspaces, pods as objects, a self-auditing knowledge graph as long-term memory, a calm day-orchestrator that knows when to leave you alone.

Tickets: every action is something you can decline

When Dayflow wants to do something on your behalf, it surfaces a ticket. Accept it and the action runs. Decline and the planner learns from it. Snooze and it'll come back later. Nothing happens without your consent at the action layer.

KG-debug room

A room where you talk to your knowledge graph directly. Inspect entities, trace evidence, find bad merges, propose repairs. The general assistant can't change the KG, but in this room the right manager team can, with audit trails.

Approval gates

Risky tools like sending email, controlling the smart home, or browser automation pause for approval before firing. You see the exact tool, the exact arguments, the full preview. Allow or Deny. Nothing acts on your behalf without an explicit yes.

Multi-surface rooms

Web UI, Slack, Telegram, SMS, code-room piped to Claude Code on your CLI. Each room has its own manager, tools, and conversation policy. Same Emi underneath; the room defines the world the agent thinks it's in.

What changes when an AI actually knows you

Most assistants forget the conversation by tomorrow. Emi's whole job is to remember, and to use what it remembers without being annoying about it.

It improves your health

Tracks your sleep, your diet, your hydration, and your energy, all from things you mention in passing, no manual logging. The belief engine notices patterns across weeks: you slept worse the nights you had wine, your headaches cluster around late nights, your focus crashes after a big lunch. It shows you the patterns. It doesn't lecture.

It knows your people

"Email Phil about Friday Night Meats." Emi already knows Phil is your friend, what his email is, what Friday Night Meats is, who else is usually invited, and the casual tone you use with that group. The KG holds your relationships, the wiki turns them into prose you can read and correct.

It learns from your reactions

Dismiss the same hydration nudge three Tuesdays in a row. Emi notices. The belief engine downgrades the pattern, the planner stops sending it. Reject a kind of suggestion and Emi gets less annoying over time, not more.

It can be brutally honest

"You're getting bad sleep. I'm cutting you off from the keyboard, go to bed." Emi has a real model of your day: when you logged in, when you went AFK, when you ate, what your calendar looks like tomorrow. It uses that model. Sometimes that means pushing back on you.

Its memory is inspectable

Emi renders your knowledge graph into a personal wiki: pages about you, your wife, your kids, your work, your friends. When something is wrong, stale, or duplicated, you can see it. Agents can fix the underlying graph with provenance. It's the only personal AI architecture we know of where memory audits itself.

It shows you everything it's doing

Other agentic systems run in the cloud and act on your behalf in ways you can't easily see. Emi inverts that. Every prompt is visible. Every tool call is visible. The exact list of tools each agent is allowed to use is in plain YAML you can read. Risky tools hit approval gates before firing. The general assistant can read your knowledge graph but cannot change it. Edits route through a separate manager team. Nothing acts on your behalf that you can't trace.

It runs on your computer

SQLite, ChromaDB, JSON resource files. Your data lives in your home directory, not on someone's servers. LLM calls go to the provider you choose (OpenAI, Gemini, Anthropic, or fully local via Ollama). No telemetry. No phone-home. Delete the folder, Emi is gone.

Under the hood

If you're a tech reader, this is where it gets interesting. EmiOS isn't just tools and memory; it's eight architectural ideas that compose into something closer to a personal operating system. Skim them; you'll find a few you haven't seen before.

The wiki is a debugger for memory

Emi builds a temporal autobiographical knowledge graph, then renders it into a personal wiki. The wiki isn't an output. It's a mirror. When a fact is stale, duplicated, or contradictory, you can see it. Agents trace the visible failure back to KG nodes, edges, and evidence, and repair the graph with provenance. Memory that audits itself.

Every life artifact is a URI

A conversation, an image, an email, a KG evidence item, a skill, a generated summary: all share the same shape: datapod:<kind>:<id>. Agents pass references, not bytes. The KG can link directly to your photos: Jukka -> depicted_in -> datapod:image:abc123. Bytes never enter prompts; tools fetch them on demand.

Rooms are bounded worlds

A room defines the world an agent thinks it's in. Each has its own manager team, tool permissions, memory scope, and conversation policy. KG-debug room. GeoGuessr room. Code room that talks to Claude Code on the CLI. Same Emi, different worlds, because the room changes the available capabilities, not the prompt.

Designed around not being annoying

Dayflow is day-level executive function. Event-driven scheduler, item state machine (new → planned → actionable → dispatched → closed → suppressed), debounced ticks, dedup'd dispatches. The loud failure mode of personal AI is over-nagging. The system was built to fail quiet, not loud.

The belief engine learns from your reactions

Daily timeline → daily insights → confidence-tracked beliefs → resource docs that every agent reads. Closed loop: dismiss the same nudge three Tuesdays in a row and the belief downgrades; the planner stops sending it. Reject a kind of suggestion and Emi gets less annoying over time, not more.

Safety by architectural separation, not by prompt

Most agentic systems "stay safe" by asking the model nicely in a system prompt. Emi enforces it structurally: per-agent tool allowlists, per-room permissions, scope contracts that can only narrow (never expand), risk-rated tools with approval gates, and a hard split between read and write managers. The general assistant can read the KG through a read-only view; it cannot write to it. Edits route through a separate manager with audit trails. Nothing acts on your behalf that you can't trace.

Fuzzy intent compiles to reliable IR

An agent can do something once. The task compiler turns the good ones into deterministic intermediate representation that runs the same way every time, auditable, editable, reusable. Agents decide what should happen; the compiler turns the proven workflows into real artifacts.

Local-first, all the way down

SQLite, ChromaDB, JSON resource files, all in your home directory. LLM calls go to the provider you pick (OpenAI, Gemini, Anthropic, or fully local via Ollama). No telemetry. No phone-home. The graph is yours; the wiki is yours; the pods are yours. MIT licensed.

One concrete walkthrough

"Find the picture of me from today and email it to Katy." Four steps, three subsystems cooperating.

1. You upload an image

The system mints an image pod with a content-addressed URI like datapod:image:9c0c…. The bytes are stored once, content-addressed; the chat history just gets the URI.

2. KG ingests the pod

The fact extractor reads the surrounding chat ("Here is a picture of me") and emits real KG edges: Jukka -> depicted_in -> datapod:image:…. The artifact is now first-class graph data.

3. Later, you ask

"Find the picture of me from today and email it to Katy." The planner does a clean two-step: a pod search returns matching pods, then Send email is called with those pods attached. The agent never had to read the bytes.

4. Send email loads the pod

The tool, not the agent, resolves the pod's address to the stored file and attaches it. Bytes never enter prompts. Provenance is preserved. The whole interaction stays composable.

Honest status

EmiOS is in private beta. Different parts are at different maturity levels. Here's the truthful map.

Stable

  • Core chat / room / agent loop
  • SQLite + ChromaDB local storage
  • Dayflow item lifecycle and tickets
  • OpenAI + Gemini + Anthropic providers
  • Pod-based image and email handling

Works but rough

  • KG → wiki rendering pipeline
  • Belief engine + daily insights
  • Multi-account Gmail / Calendar OAuth
  • Slack / Telegram / SMS rooms
  • Smart-home integrations (Nest, Ring)

Experimental

  • Self-healing KG repair loop
  • Playwright browser automation
  • Skill learning from traces
  • Pod cross-agent routing patterns
  • Custom orchestrators beyond Dayflow

Heads-up

  • Defaults bind to localhost; do not expose port 8000
  • Cloud LLMs receive prompt context that may include personal data; local data is never uploaded wholesale
  • Approval gates exist for risky tools but rely on you reading them
